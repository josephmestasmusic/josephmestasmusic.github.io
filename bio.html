Biography

Joseph Mestas is an innovative, collaborative saxophonist, private instructor, and mathematician originally from Aurora, Colorado. He has studied with saxophonists Aaron Burr and Andrew Dahlke at the University of Northern Colorado in Greeley. Having been playing the saxophone since the age of 11, he has performed with many wind bands and chamber ensembles such as the University of Northern Colorado Wind Ensemble and the “Pair of Reeds” Saxophone Duet. He is a two-time Southard Competition finalist and performed for a masterclass with saxophonist Lucas Hopkins.

He strives for the expansion of new instrumental contemporary repertoire for all levels of musicians as it is essential for both performers and audiences to understand the importance of instruments and instrumental music in acoustic spaces.

Joseph is an undergraduate at the University of Northern Colorado pursuing degrees in Music Business and Mathematics alongside a certificate in Arts Entrepreneurship. He is currently planning his first recital in early 2024 (date TBD) featuring music by Erwin Schulhoff, Paul Bonneau, and Alexander Glazunov.
